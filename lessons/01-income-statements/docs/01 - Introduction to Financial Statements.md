## Introduction to financial statements

You learned about the basics of financial statements, focusing on the income statement, which details sales, costs, and profits. Understanding these concepts is crucial for financial forecasting. Here are the key points you covered:

* **Financial Statements:** Records outlining the financial activities of a business, providing a clear view of its financial health.
* **Types of Financial Statements:**
  * **Income Statement:** Shows income and expenses, leading to profit or loss.
  * **Balance Sheet:** Lists assets, liabilities, and capital. 
* **Gross Profit:** Calculated as total sales minus the cost of goods sold (COGS). It indicates the core profitability of a product.
  * **Formula:** Gross Profit = Total Sales - Total COGS
* **Net Profit:** Gross profit minus operating expenses (OpEx), including costs like admin, marketing, and insurance.
  * **Formula:** Net Profit = Gross Profit - OpEx

* Example Code:
```
# Create and print the opex list
opex = [admin, travel, training, marketing, insurance]
print(opex)

# Calculate and print net profit
net_profit = gross_profit - sum(opex)
print("The net profit is {}.".format(net_profit))
```
